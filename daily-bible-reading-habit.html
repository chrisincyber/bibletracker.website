How to Start a Daily Bible Reading Habit That Sticks

January 15, 2025 8 min read

Starting a daily Bible reading habit can feel overwhelming. With 66 books, 1,189 chapters, and over 31,000 verses, where do you even begin? The good news is that building this life-changing habit is simpler than you might think.

Whether you've tried before and struggled, or you're just beginning your journey with Scripture, these proven strategies will help you develop a consistent Bible reading practice that transforms your relationship with God.

Why Daily Bible Reading Matters

Before diving into the "how," let's remember the "why." Scripture isn't just another book to check off your reading list. It's described as:

  • Living and active (Hebrews 4:12) - God's Word has power to transform
  • A lamp to our feet (Psalm 119:105) - It guides our daily decisions
  • Spiritual nourishment (Matthew 4:4) - We need it like we need food

Daily engagement with Scripture isn't about religious duty - it's about relationship. Just as you'd naturally want to communicate daily with someone you love, reading the Bible is how we hear from God.

1. Start Small (Really Small)

One of the biggest mistakes people make is trying to read too much too soon. Reading three chapters a day sounds noble, but it often leads to burnout and guilt when you miss a day.

Instead, try this: Commit to reading just one verse per day for the first week. Yes, one verse. The goal isn't to impress anyone - it's to build the habit of opening your Bible daily.

"Your word is a lamp for my feet, a light on my path."

- Psalm 119:105

Once one verse becomes automatic, gradually increase to a paragraph, then a chapter. This "tiny habits" approach works because it removes the psychological barrier of starting.

2. Anchor It to an Existing Habit

The most successful habit-builders attach new habits to existing ones. This is called "habit stacking."

Examples:

  • After I pour my morning coffee, I will read one chapter of the Bible
  • After I brush my teeth at night, I will read a Psalm
  • During my lunch break, I will read for 10 minutes

The key is consistency in timing. Your brain will begin to associate the trigger (coffee, teeth brushing, lunch) with Bible reading, making it automatic over time.

3. Create a Dedicated Space

Environment matters more than willpower. Having a specific place for Bible reading signals to your brain that it's time to focus on God's Word.

This doesn't need to be elaborate - a favorite chair, a corner of your kitchen table, or even a park bench on your morning walk. Keep your Bible (or phone with a Bible app) readily accessible in that space.

4. Use a Reading Plan

A reading plan removes the daily decision of "what should I read?" Decision fatigue is real, and eliminating this barrier makes consistency much easier.

Popular reading plan options:

  • Chronological - Read events in the order they happened
  • Gospel-focused - Start with Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John
  • Psalms and Proverbs - Read one Psalm and one Proverb daily
  • New Testament first - Great for new believers
  • One Year Bible - Old Testament, New Testament, Psalms, and Proverbs daily

5. Track Your Progress

There's something powerful about seeing your progress. Tracking creates accountability and provides motivation when you see your streak building.

This is exactly why we built YourBibleTracker - to help you visualize your journey through Scripture. Seeing which books you've completed and tracking your reading streaks provides that extra motivation to keep going.

6. Embrace Imperfection

You will miss days. Life happens - illness, travel, emergencies, or simply forgetting. The key is how you respond.

When you miss a day:

  • Don't try to "catch up" by doubling the next day (this leads to burnout)
  • Don't beat yourself up or spiral into guilt
  • Simply start again the next day

Remember: God's grace covers your imperfect attempts. He's delighted that you want to know Him through His Word, regardless of your consistency rate.

7. Engage, Don't Just Read

Bible reading becomes a habit when it becomes meaningful. Passive reading often feels like a chore, but active engagement transforms it into a conversation with God.

Try these engagement techniques:

  • Journaling - Write down one thing that stood out to you
  • Prayer - Turn what you read into a prayer
  • Application - Ask "How does this apply to my life today?"
  • Memorization - Choose one verse per week to memorize

8. Find Community

Reading the Bible with others provides accountability and deeper understanding. Consider:

  • Joining a small group or Bible study
  • Reading the same passage as a friend and discussing it
  • Sharing insights with your spouse or family

Getting Started Today

Don't wait for the perfect moment or the perfect plan. Start today with these simple steps:

  1. Choose a time (morning, lunch, or evening)
  2. Pick a starting point (John is great for beginners)
  3. Commit to just 5 minutes
  4. Track your progress with an app like YourBibleTracker

The journey through Scripture is one of the most rewarding adventures you'll ever take. Every day you open God's Word, you're positioning yourself to hear from the Creator of the universe. That's an incredible privilege.

Start small, stay consistent, and watch how daily time in God's Word transforms your life.
